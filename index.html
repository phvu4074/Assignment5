Instructions

  • The switch control that I implemented is a 3 button one which can be found at the bottom of the calculator.
  • To initiat the switch control, simply press either the "Previous" button or the "Next" button and the number "7" will be highlighted.
  • Pressing the "Next" button will highlight the next button of the row.
  • Pressing the "Previous" button will highlight the previous button of the row.
  • Pressing the "Select" button will select the currently highlighted button.

Tests

  • I tested the time it took to enter 5 equations using my right hand compared to left hand.
  • The equations I used were: 99/44/99, 78/56, (7+6)/3, 30/3*10-60, and 88*54-24+111
  • Using my right hand I managed to to finish entering the 5 equations in 2:07.71 minutes, 2:04.06 minutes, and 1:52.09 minutes over the courae of three tries
  • Using my left hand I managed to to finish entering the 5 equations in 2:23.71 minutes, 2:40.86 minutes, and 2:10.52 minutes over the courae of three tries
  • The results do not suprise me as my right hand is my dominant hand and thus should be faster than left hand. I found that the mistake that I made most constantly when entering equations is simply overshooting the button I wanted to enter becuase of overspamming either the "Previous" or "Next" button. This fact makes me realise the how limiting the number of buttons can drastically decrease efficiency. In the case of my switch ui, I can at least push "Previous" or "Next" to correct overshooting a button. However, in the case of a 2 button ui with only "Next" and "Select" the only way to correct overshooting is literally cycling through the entire calculator until they return to the button they want to press. This makes it abundantly clear the speed difference between the three button and two button switch control.
